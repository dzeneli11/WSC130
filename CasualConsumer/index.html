Our mission at Casual Consumer is to help the general public make more intelligent decisions about food both inside and outside of their lives.

We review different kinds of foods ranging to cross-cultural and traditional American cusines. We want to inform you which food is worth trying in New York, and how it can it bring great expierences to the community surrounding it.

"Chip"

The new cookie shop has recently opened in Astoria, reviews are very postive since they're renowned for the fresh hot cookies they make on the spot, flavors range from regular, Birthday, S'mores, Double Chocolate Chunk, & more.

"SolBol"

A very new startup that has a delightful taste when it comes to Acai bowls, and smoothies. You have a choice of adding many toppings to your fruit bowls like Honey nut peanut butter, Pumpkin spice, Cookie dough and much more. The CEO is higly energetic and caring for every customer that he serves, and encounters.

"Ample Hills Creamery"

When it comes to Ice cream Ample is king, There are various of flavors and endless customization of toppings to your liking, ranging from hot fudge, shredded cookie dough, and caramel swirl. Their sucess has lead them to start selling their own ice cream to local markets aroudn new york.

"BareBurger"

100% grass fed burgers they say, though the taste just isn't there, if your looking for good burgers this isnt the place you want to go to. "My burger didn't have pickles and half the fries in the order were cold. One order was nice and hot, the other, obviously cold. Meat in the build your own cheeseburger was a little underdone" - Quote from "KeepItReal I." (Yelp)

Smorgasburg is the largest weekly open-air food market in America, attracting 20,000-30,000 people to Brooklyn each weekend to eat from 100 local vendors.

Their locations vary though its every spring in Williamsburg, ranging from various fresh cross-cultural food served on the spot made right in front of your eyes

"Kylclades Mediterranean Seafood"

Seafood in new york has been let down in some boroughs, but not this one. Taverna Kyclades, in Astoria Queens, is one of the top Greek restaurants in New York City. Since they launched fresh food is their number one priority, made with the best ingredients based on original Greek recipes.

"Royal 35 Steakhouse"

Steak is the name of the game when it comes to dry aged Prime beef aged for 30-35 days. They also specialize in their own wines and have a wide range iof high quality food when it comes to serving. The french onion soup has amazing texture in combination with their seafood tower, they have endless creativity when it comes to fancy styled food.

"Katz Delicatessen"

This deli heavily known for its pastrami and beef had been serving its meats since 1888. Each week thousands of vistors dine in to eat at this legendary deli. This whole business was built on quality charged 22$ for a single pastrami sandwich. Some people believe they have forgotten their roots since they have upped the prices due to heavy tourism near the location.

"Nick's Pizza"

This pizza is highly renown for its classic thin crisp, and it had attracted many because of this pizza review, the business had started since 1993 and is said to be one of the best pizzerias in Queens NY.
